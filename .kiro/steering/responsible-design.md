# Responsible Language Guidelines

## Risk Level Labels

All risk communication in the app MUST use these labels exclusively:

- **"lower-risk"** — exposure score ≤ 50
- **"higher-risk"** — exposure score 51–75
- **"not recommended"** — exposure score > 75

## Forbidden Language

- NEVER use the word **"safe"** in any user-facing label, description, recommendation, or output string.
- NEVER make medical claims or guarantee safety.
- Avoid absolute language like "guaranteed", "completely", "100% protected".

## Required Disclaimers

- The Day Planner page MUST display a prototype disclaimer stating the planner uses demo data and estimated calculations for educational and planning purposes only.
- Results pages MUST note that all campus data is manually seeded for the hackathon and does not reflect real-time conditions.
- The Methodology page MUST state the planner is for planning and awareness purposes and is not a substitute for official heat safety guidance.

## Recommendation Tone

- Frame recommendations as options, not directives: "Consider leaving earlier" not "You must leave earlier".
- Use "may help reduce exposure" rather than "will keep you safe".
- Always pair risk assessments with actionable alternatives.
